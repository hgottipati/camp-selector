Booking a family camping spot is actually pretty straightforward once you know which system to use + when to book. Here’s a clean breakdown (especially for around Seattle/Washington 👇)

🏕️ 1. Pick WHERE you want to camp

There are 3 main types:

🟢 State Parks (easiest for families)
Example: Deception Pass, Lake Wenatchee
Use:
👉 Washington State Parks reservation system

✔ Good bathrooms, safe, kid-friendly
✔ Car camping (drive to site)

🟢 National Parks / Forests
Example: Olympic National Park, Mt. Baker
Use:
👉 Recreation.gov

✔ More scenic, more “nature”
✔ Slightly more planning needed

🟢 Private campgrounds (KOA, etc.)
Book directly on their websites
✔ Easiest (often playgrounds, showers, etc.)
📅 2. When to book (this is key)
Washington State Parks open ~9 months in advance
Popular summer weekends = book the day it opens
Same-day bookings are possible until ~2 PM if available

👉 Rule of thumb:

Summer weekends → book early (like booking flights)
Shoulder season → you can be more relaxed
💻 3. How to actually book (step-by-step)
For State Parks:
Go to Washington reservation site
Select:
Dates
Number of people
Tent / RV
Pick a campsite from the map
Pay and confirm

✔ You can reserve up to 2 sites in one booking

For National Parks:
Go to Recreation.gov
Search the park (e.g., Olympic)
Pick campground → site → reserve

✔ Thousands of sites across the US in one place

👨‍👩‍👦 4. What to choose for family camping

Look for:

“Standard campsite” or “Family campsite”
Close to:
Restrooms 🚽
Water 💧
Parking 🚗

Avoid:

“Walk-in” or “backcountry” (hard with kids)
⚡ Pro tips (this is where people mess up)
Check-in time matters (usually afternoon)
Weekends sell out fast (especially near Seattle)
Some parks switch to first-come-first-served in winter
Always have a backup campground
